Frequently Asked Questions

What is SkillLoop?
A structured digital platform that helps teenagers build real-world life skills across four areas: physical coordination, independence skills, social skills, and executive function — through short video lessons, practice, quizzes, and written reflections.
Who is it for?
Teens roughly 12–18, including students with IEPs, 504 plans, ADHD, autism, or other developmental needs — and any teen who wants a clearer system for learning everyday skills.
Which modules are available right now?
Physical Coordination is live, with lessons releasing in sequence. Independence Skills, Social Skills, and Executive Function are in production, along with the Avatar Shop and Game Mode shown on your dashboard.
What are stars?
Every completed assignment earns 1 star. Stars bank automatically and will be spendable on avatar outfits and accessories when the Avatar Shop launches — nothing you earn now is lost.
What does the progress wheel show?
Each colored quarter is one module. A quarter only fills when you actually complete assignments in that module — it starts blank and reflects real progress only.
Why does the dashboard mention active days?
Consistent practice is what builds skills, so we track the days you use the platform. For families participating through a care team, regular monthly engagement also supports the check-ins and documentation their team maintains.
How much does it cost?
Standard is $25/month and Premium is $50/month, cancel anytime. Families participating through a partnered care team may access SkillLoop at no direct cost, where their team's services are covered by insurance — eligibility is determined by the care team.
Is my data private?
Yes. No ads, no selling data, per-user access controls, and encryption in transit and at rest. See the Privacy Policy for the full picture, including how COPPA and school-related data are handled.
Who grades the written reflections?
Reflections receive instant supportive feedback generated automatically. They are also stored so that, for families working with a care team, a real clinician can review them — the automatic feedback never replaces human judgment.
How do I get help?
Email help@skillloop.org and we will get back to you.
